The problem

Job searching is broken by default

Most people send the same CV to every job, apply without knowing if they're qualified, and lose track of every application they've made. It's exhausting — and it produces poor results. Radar fixes all three.

01
You apply blind
You read a job description but have no way to know how well you actually match it. You either over-apply to roles where you have no chance, or under-apply to ones you'd be perfect for.
02
Your CV is generic
Tailoring a CV properly for every role takes hours. So most people send the same one. Hiring managers see it — and the application goes straight to the bottom of the pile.
03
You lose track
After ten applications, you can't remember who you applied to, what you said, or what stage you're at. Following up feels awkward because the context is lost.
How it works

Three steps to a smarter job search

Radar turns the chaos of job hunting into a structured, intelligent process — from the moment you see a posting to the day you sign an offer.

01
Build your profile once
Upload your CV and add your experience. Radar reads and understands your background — skills, sector expertise, education. The richer your profile, the sharper every analysis becomes.
02
Analyse any job posting instantly
Select a role from APEC, LinkedIn, Indeed, Welcome to the Jungle, or paste any job description. Radar scores your fit, identifies your strengths and gaps, and tells you exactly how to position yourself.
03
Apply with a CV written for that role
Radar generates a professional LaTeX CV tailored to the specific posting — not a generic template. Every application goes out with the best possible version of your story for that opportunity.
04
Track every application in one place
Log each application, update its status as things progress, add notes from interviews, and see all your opportunities mapped geographically. Nothing falls through the cracks.
